City skylines around the world has been changing very fast lately, and many new buildings is appearing in places that only few years ago had empty land. Architects says that the demand for modern offices and apartments are increasing, but sometimes the planning and the designs goes through many problems before they are finished.

In several major cities, developers have been constructing tall glass towers that tries to look futuristic, but not all of them succeed to impress the public. Some residents complain that the buildings blocks sunlight from nearby streets, while others saying that the design feels too similar to ones that was already built before.

One example comes from a recently opened residential complex that include three towers connected by sky bridges. The project was promoted as an innovative solution for urban living, however many critics argue the buildings does not blend with the historical neighborhoods around it. “It look modern but also strangely out of place,” one local resident said, adding that the project were approved too quickly.

Another development that has gained attention are a mixed-use building with offices, shops and apartments in the same structure. City officials claims that these kind of buildings helps reduce commuting times, but some transportation experts believes the area infrastructure are not ready to support the additional traffic.

Construction companies also faces rising costs and shortages of materials, which have caused delays in several projects that was expected to open earlier this year. Because of these issues, some developers is reconsidering their original designs or reducing the height of planned towers.

Despite the challenges, many cities continues to push forward with ambitious construction plans. Urban planners argues that denser developments are necessary to support growing populations, although critics warn that poorly planned projects could create long-term problems for communities.

As more buildings gets announced and constructed, debates about architecture, urban planning and sustainability is likely to continue, especially as residents and officials tries to balance growth with preserving the character of their cities.
